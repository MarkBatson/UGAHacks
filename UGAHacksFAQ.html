PARTNERS

  1. Who is eligible to join?

    2. All university students! We welcome all undergraduates and graduate students to attend. Professionals and other guests are welcome to attend as mentors/volunteers. If you're a high schooler who wishes to attend, please email us at hello@ugahacks.com for further information. Please be sure to bring your student ID or government issued ID (no photo copies allowed).

  2. I want to volunteer and/or become a mentor!

    3. Great! We love your excitement! Applications for volunteering/mentoring will be released later in the semester so check back soon.

  3. How many people can I have on my team?

    4. There's no hard limit but we recommend teams be at most 4 people.

  4. What are the prizes and how can I win them?

    5. Prizes will be a surprise :) and you can win them by participating in sponsored challenges - which will be announced at the hackathon - or by winning the judges' interest in your project.

  5. Where can I sleep?

    6. We'll have designated sleeping areas for hackers but they will be on a first come first serve basis. You will not allowed to reserve or save a space.

  6. Do you offer travel reimbursement?

    7. Please state that you are seeking a travel reimbursement on your application. Travel reimbursement will be issued on a case by case basis.

  7. How much does this event cost me?

    8. Nothing! The entire event and its amazing perks are free for all participants.

  8. Will I have to bring food?

    9. You can if you want but we'll have food readily available for hackers. Please note that if you require any specific diets, we are not able to guarantee those accommodations.

  9. What if I don't have a team or idea?

    10. Don't sweat it! You're free to join any existing team or form a new one when you get there. We'll also provide ample opportunities for hackers to meet each other and brainstorm some amazing ideas.

  10. Can I start working on my project before the event?

    11. No. All UGAHacks projects must begin at the hackathon. You are not permitted to begin a hackathon project before this event.

  11. What should I bring?

    12. Laptops, phones, chargers, and other tools you might need for your project. Additionally, please bring your official student ID or government ID (no photo copies allowed).

  12. What's the Code of Conduct?

    13. You can find out more by visiting the MLH Code of Conduct page

  13. I have more questions!

    14. We'd love to answer them. Feel free to drop us an email at hello@ugahacks.com.
